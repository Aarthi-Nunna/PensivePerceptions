  • Borrowed Time

    Sand trickled down the narrow path
    Time ticked with every passing grain
    One bulb turned empty, the other turned full
    As time ticked by mercilessly

    Every grain turned into that of lost time
    Every grain relished the present turning into the past
    But would it stay hidden and beneath the sands of the present?
    And pave way for old and bygone memories to be cherished
    Or would it hide in plain sight above the sands of time?

    Fresh and raw from its smiles and hidden scars
    Every granule would bid it's goodbye
    Falling through its chosen path effortlessly
    So would every second of time pass by
    without even a final adieu

    The sand was however finite,
    Eventually it would stop falling and running
    Time was limited and it would outrun us all
    And hence would come a point,
    A point where no more sand is left to trickle down

    And that is when the hourglass is turned
    And time would start ticking again
    But this time would be different from before
    As we would be living on borrowed time


  • Innocence

    She wandered the streets, a small ingenuous child,
    Streets purporting promises and delicacies of nature offered in mere pence's
    Awestruck by the hues of blue skies and grey cobble muddled together
    A blind naïve eye she had cast on the strings knotted wrong

    She inhaled nature in its purest and rawest form,
    Filtering everything that had sinister visiting its vicinity
    She listened to the sound of nature in all its glory, mere background to an unkeen listener
    A deaf ear she had cast to the words of truth and ugly

    And she grew a little older with every step she took in the street she called home,
    A little less naïve, a little more blossomed and mature
    A blind eye eventually turned all seeing
    A deaf ear unknowingly turned conscious and hearing

    The blues of the sky turned sombre with clouds of grey obscuring the stars
    The grey cobbled street seemed to furl into black tar, not a soul left to dance upon it
    The wrongly knit strings stared blaringly back at her in contrast
    Why had the street once home turned foreign with time?

    Nature she had turned to for warmth and comfort seemed null,
    Fragrance of purity lost in the decay of liveliness and the presence of responsibilities
    Sounds of nature discordant with the voices of mankind and internal quarrels
    The innocence of a child’s eye, ignorance a true bliss


  • The Shadow of Light

    I sat on an abandoned bench adjacent to a lamp,
    the Sun was asleep and the moon half awake
    The wood felt cold against my bare skin
    Along with the mounds of snow that erased the tracks beneath
    But the light of the lamp was dull and innocent
    Giving out warmth against the cold night sky

    I looked at the bulb of the lone lamp post
    Flickering and emitting a dull hue of yellow
    Looking above i met the gaze of countless stars
    Shining bright and stealing the light of the sole crescent

    Neither of them seemed to complain....
    About their loneliness or the fear of being overshadowed
    Neither seemed to mind
    The cloudy ether or the misty overcast

    Looking down at the white cold blanket
    I brushed away the snow to find some earth
    Finding myself a silhouette, pale and sombre
    Complemented by both the stars and the lamp

    Maybe too many cooks didn't have to spoil the broth
    Maybe being alone wasn't a thing to shower pity on or mourn
    Shining alone or as together as one didn't matter
    As both had paved way to the shadows down beneath


  • Jar of Fireflies

    Illuminating the smallest of space that surrounds you
    Magnificent a sight it would be to see you through a droplet or dew
    Have you ever wondered why you've been given the gift of light?
    Have you ever wondered how you are such a beautiful sight?

    Your wings seem to capture our every emotion
    And does your light flicker with every thought and notion?
    You've been confined to the walls of this jar
    Regardless you fight and glow bright just like a star

    Am sorry to have captured you
    But I have a few thoughts I have pondered and want to share with you
    You've managed to shine bright even on the darkest day
    You've had an undying spirit during the grey

    How has my confinement not rendered you hopeless?
    How have you managed to survive through my mess?
    Dear jar of fireflies, I hope you burn with the same courage and light that will never die
    For your light is my inspiration that I can burn bright during the darkest of times, or atleast try

    A jar of fireflies
    A mind full of thoughts
    A heart full of emotions
    A soul full of life
